Structure of Water

  • Water has a bent structure
  • The Oxygen atom has two lone pairs of electrons
  • The Oxygen atom is sp3 hybridized (VSEPR model)
  • Along with lone pairs and hydrogen atom the water molecule has tetrahedron geometery

Hydrogen Bonding in Water

  • Each water molecule can form Four Hydrogen bonds
  • Two Hydrgogen bonds are formed through Oxygen atom as an Acceptor
  • Two Hydrogen bonds are formed through the two Hydrogen Atoms as Donor
  • The four hydrogen bonded water molecules are also arranged in a tetrahedron geometry

Phases of Water

  • In gas phase water molecules are moving at very high speed forming hydrogen bonds only in clusters
  • In Liquid phase, the hydrogen bonds undergo continuous formation and breakage as water molecules are randomly moving
  • In solid phase (ice) water molecules form crystal in which each water molecule is hydrogen bonded to four other water molecules
